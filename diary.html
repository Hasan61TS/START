Research Log: Day 1

January 15, 1983

Another cold day in Novosibirsk. The lab feels different today - warmer, despite the Siberian winter outside. I've finally done it. The initial tests on the sunlight alteration device were successful. The way it breaks down pure solar radiation into its components is unlike anything I've seen before.

Father's lectures about excellence echo in my mind. This could be my chance to prove myself, to win the National Technological Advancement Competition. To finally break free from his shadow. Mother seems proud, though she's always been the gentler one, my refuge when Father's expectations become too heavy.

The possibilities are endless. Clean energy, enhanced agriculture, a chance to heal this broken world. I have to be careful though - such power needs to be handled responsibly.

Research Log: Day 140

March 30, 1983

They ran an "authorized test" today without my knowledge or consent. The results were... disturbing. The lab rats in the radiation chamber didn't just show accelerated growth - their behavior changed. They became docile, almost robotic. Following simple commands without resistance. When I questioned this, Mother smiled that same cold smile: "Isn't it beautiful? Perfect harmony through perfect obedience."

I found more records. Project DAWN wasn't just about solar energy. Grandfather wasn't trying to harness the sun's power - he was trying to stop someone from weaponizing it. The same someone who now controls my research. I have to find out what happened to him. Before history repeats itself.
